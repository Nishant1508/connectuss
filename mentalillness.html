Seminar on Mental Health Issues

Tanmay Bhatt

“Advice is a form of Nostalgia”

-The Sunscreen Song

A seminar regarding ‘Issues Related to Mental Health in a College Setting’ was conducted in the C Block Auditorium on the 2nd of September. Students were educated about various mental health issues that are commonly observed among youth and encouraged to seek treatment.

The seminar was conducted by the Manas Foundation which was represented by Ms. Ujwalla, a psychologist and Ms. Suranjana, a clinical psychologist. The Manas Foundation has previously been in the news for its “Mera Imaan , Mahilaon ka Samman” campaign organised in association with Auto-rickshaw drivers of Delhi-NCR. They are also involved in interventional activities related to gender justice and child protection among others.

The event started with a quick round of introductions in which all audience members including faculty took part. Students were then asked to speak on the differences between college and school life. This was facilitated by a ‘Firecracker activity’ for which a rugby ball was thrown at random among students, with the student coming into its possession being required to share his experiences related to the topic discussed. This activity allowed attendees to speak more candidly and frankly, with problems pertaining to social adjustments, time management and communication issues among peers being discussed.

The speakers further led discussion about stereotypes in peer groups, and thought processing under stress. A session in which students analysed and revised past stressful experiences was conducted, anxiety was talked of as an extreme manifestation of stress, prompting intelligent responses from the students. Ms. Ujwalla further elucidated on the social issues surrounding mental illness, “People often do not seek clinical assistance due to fear of social stigma, acceptance of mental illnesses in the society is essential as these are more common than they are perceived to be.”

The importance of a positive psychological outlook was highlighted, so as to prevent maladaptive practices like imprudent usage of drugs and sexual activity. These can provide temporary relief but end up compromising mental capabilities. In the end, “Everyone is free to wear sunscreen” a spoken word song by Baz Luhrmann was played, an introspective and inspiring end to a highly informative seminar.
