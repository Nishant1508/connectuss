Trip to Delhi Secretariat

Tanmay Bhatt ,Azain Ahmed

“Advice is a form of Nostalgia”

-The Sunscreen Song

On the 9th of September, a delegation of 17 students and 2 staff members was sent to the Delhi Secretariat to witness the launch of the "Higher education and skill development guarantee scheme" of the Delhi government.

The trip was organized by the DSW, and included students who had been nominated by their respective schools to be among the first recipients of the loan.

Vice-Chancellors and Registrars from various institutes of Delhi-NCT were also in attendance along with students. Mr. Manish Sisodia (deputy CM) , Mr. P. K. Sharma(PNB), Mr. Punya S Srivastava (Secy HE/TTE), Mr. K. K. Sharma (Chief Secretary), Mr. S. N. Sahai(Principal Secretary Finance), Mr. Naveen Kundra (Convener SLBC) and Dr. Ram S Sangapure (Ex-director PNB) were the main speakers at the event. The event began with a brief introduction of the scheme presented by a member of the Secretariat. This scheme has been started as a means to fulfill the current AAP government's primary goals of higher education improvement.

For this purpose a trust has been made with the deputy CM as chairperson to provide corpus to the government for dispensing loans. Other official posts are held by the Education Minister and the Principal Secretary of Finance.

10cr is the initial corpus set up, with plans to expand it to 30cr. Since this is a universal scheme, all students who are pursuing any higher educational qualification be it diploma, degree, vocational course etc are eligible to receive this loan. The only prerequisite is that the student must have passed the 10th and 12th examinations from a school in Delhi for courses requiring 12th pass, and for courses requiring only 10th exam qualification the same must also have been passed in DELHI-NCT. The scheme covers all government institutes and private institutions with fees regulated by the SFRC or those having been accredited an A or A+ rating by the NAAC or NBA.
Base interest rates shall be around 2% and will vary depending on the bank.
Students will have a moratorium period of one year after completion of their course in which no repayment is required, only after this shall repayment be required.
Additional interest relaxation will be provided to women students. Applicants for the loan will be required to submit their UIDAI (Aadhar) details, and where applicable the details for their parents as well.
CIBIL scores of the defaulting students and their parents will be adversely effected.
In case of rejection of loan application, a complaint can be made to the committee for grievance redressal that shall be set up by the DHE.

Mr Manish Sisodia, the Deputy Chief Minister of Delhi said that the scheme is made keeping in mind all the students needs at the launch of the scheme.. It not only includes the tuition fee but other expenses too such as hostel fee. He said the scheme is universal in nature and emphasised that it can only be made successfull if there is trust between the students and the Government .

The programme ended with Mr Manish Sisodia distributing checks to the students of various educational institutes.
