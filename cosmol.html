Workshop on COMSOL Multiphysics

error

On the 29th of August, starting at 2:00 PM a basic workshop on COMSOL MULTIPHYSICS, was conducted in C-Block Auditorium. The event was organized by the students of REACT, the technical society of University School of Chemical Technology. COMSOL is an interactive software that provides an environment for modeling and simulating scientific and engineering problems. The speaker at the workshop was Dr.Samarth Agarwal, an alumnus of IIT Bombay with a doctorate from Purdue University. He was assisted by Mr.Hemant Kumar, Engineer at Technical Sales Department, COMSOL Inc.

The event started with an introduction of the software, in which Dr.Agarwal spoke at length about the salient features and conveniences of COMSOL. The chief difference between other simulators and COMSOL being the ability of COMSOL to develop self-sufficient apps that can allow end users to specify parameters and observe simulations according to their requirements without being involved with back-end programming. Various applications of COMSOL in educational, research and scientific contexts were outlined in this session. This was followed by a brief recess of 15 minutes during which tea and snacks were served.

After the intermission, COMSOL servers, a method for app distribution within a network, were described to the students briefly before beginning the hands-on session.A simple implementation of COMSOL for an electrothermal system was demonstrated using a projector, and students prepared with COMSOL installed laptops replicated the steps for their first experience using COMSOL.Results from the simulation were received with ease, with queries from students being quickly resolved by Mr.Kumar.

Dr.Agarwal continued the workshop with a brief talk about various licenses available for use by COMSOL, another advantage of COMSOL being the ability to provide Named-user locked, CPU locked and floating Network based license. Unlike many other companies, COMSOL provides perpetual licenses with fees being charged for tech support and upgrades only after the first year.

Dr.Agrawal concluded by opening the floor for a Q and A session and provided contact details for any doubts that may arise later during the student’s COMSOL usage.
Dr. Samarth Agrawal ------ samarth@comsol.com
Mr.Hemant Kumar ------- hemant@comsol.com

The event was a success with a large turnout of students, the auditorium filled beyond capacity. This was largely due to hard work put in by the students of REACT and the enthusiastic response of the students of USCT.
