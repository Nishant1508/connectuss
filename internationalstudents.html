INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS' MEET

No name

The Office of International Affairs organized the "International students' Meet 2015" on 18th September, 2015 in GGSIPU. It was held to welcome the international students newly admitted to the various programmes in the university and its affiliated colleges. The event took off with a welcome speech by Prof. Anu Singh Lather, Director, International Affairs. She welcomed all the new internationsl students in the university. They all come from various parts of the world. She shared that the current strength of international students in the university is 107. These are the students who are currently admitted in the various programmes of the university. Also, a total of 97 students are there in the affiliated colleges of the GGSIP university. She aslo shared that steps will be taken accordingly in the future. That would be in order to ensure more and more international students are motivated to opt for higher studies in GGSIPU.

After the welcome speech the cultural performances started. International Students' performed and first performance was a Nepali song by Smita, student of USS. This was followed by a Tibetan dance performamce by a group of tibetian students.

Then there wa s guest lecture on "Indian as an Education Destination for Nepalese Students" by Mr. Krishna Prasad Panthar, Cultural Counsellor, Embassy of Nepal. He shared his views on the same. Also, he sang a Nepali song which was enjoyed by everyone.

It was followed by a guest lecture by Mr. Safiullah Delawar, Second Secretary, Embassy of Afghanistan. The lecture was on "Potential for Educational Collaboration between India and Afghanistan". He shared that Afghanstan students liked being in India. He also shared various concerns faced by Afghanistan students like the compulsory Demand Draft that they need to make in order to pay fees. And how it is sometimes difficult to arrange for a demand draft as banks sometimes refuse to make them.

This was followed by guest lecture on "Role of Education in Human Life" by Acharya Yeshi Phuntsok, Member, Tibet Parliament in Exile. He shared that he felt it very important to not miss this Meet. He told that he was coming straight from an International Conference being held in Dharamsala, Himachal Pradesh. And that he would be leaving for back to Dharamsala the same night. He shared his views on the importance of Education. How education turns a person into a different human being. It changes how our mind percieves the things around us.

Then momentos were presented to the dignitaries. And then the cultural performances followed which included various performances by the international students. Like the Chammak Challo song, a few English songs by students from Nepal and Tibet. Also, female studetns from Nepal performed Nepali dance. It was followed by zombie dance.

Then the evening ended by a vote of thanks by Dr. Gagandeep Sharma, Associate Director, International Affairs. He thanked all the guests, professors and students for attending the event. Also shared his concerns shared by the guest lecturers and that they would be addressed and taken into consideration in the future.
