APJ event

no name

Where there is righteousness in the heart, there is beauty in the character. When there is beauty in the character, there is harmony in the home. When there is harmony in the home, there is order in the nation. When there is order in the Nation, there is peace in the world."
–APJ Abdul Kalam at EU golden jubilee in 2007

On the occasion of the birth anniversary of the ‘Missile Man’, late Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, Biotechnology Research and Information Society (BRaINS) of USBT organized a seminar for the students of USBT, ‘Remembering Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam’ on 14 October in E-Block auditorium. The event was a commemoration of the ‘People’s President’ Dr. Kalam, and also aimed at enlightening the students with the various ideals which Dr. Kalam used to preach. It was marked by passage reading, essay writing, short film screening, and poster making. Unlike most of the weekly talks and conferences held each Wednesday for the students of USBT, this event was organized solely by the BRaINS keeping in tone with a Debate on ‘Genome Editing’ held last week.

The seminar commenced with an inaugural address by Prof. N. Raghuram, Dean, USBT, who mentioned the need for us to put an end to stereotyping of various religions, caste, etc. “The very people that honoured Kalam by making him president also insulted him by using the label ‘Muslim’ to describe him.” Dr. Raghuram said. “A man should be known by his achievements rather than by his surname, religion or regional identity.” The inauguration speech was followed by a short film on Dr. Kalam’s life. Students of USBT then joined in by reading out various passages from books authored by Dr. Kalam whereas Ruchika, a student from M.tech, recited a poem written by Dr. Kalam.

With ‘Ignited Minds’, one of Dr. Kalam’s bestsellers, as the motivation, BRaINS had organized an essay writing competition wherein the participants had to submit an essay on the topic: ‘Who is the enemy of higher education in India?’ Even on a short notice, the competition saw massive participation and the entries came flooding in, making it a baffling task for the judges to pick a single winner. Appourva Sharad Gupta, from M.Tech (FBT) 1st year won the competition for her essay, which contained about 2000 words. Students had made sketches and posters containing quotes by the Missile Man, and also a replica for Agni missile, one of the missiles whose development was pioneered by Dr. Abdul Kalam.

Another short film was screened which showed parts of different speeches by late Dr. Abdul Kalam, who passed away on 27 July 2015 due to sudden a cardiac arrest. Urging the youth to be the change, the words were just as inspiring as reading the Ignited Minds itself. After the movie, Prof. K. Kannan of USBT finally addressed the audience with his personal experience of meeting Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam during the latter’s tenure as the President of India upon being appointed as the Vice-Chancellor of Nagaland University. Prof. Kannan was conferred the title by Dr. Kalam in the year 2006.

After being conducted in a well-structured manner, the seminar concluded with a vote of thanks given by the President of BRaINS, Abhishek Pateria, who thanked the students of first and second years in B.Tech (Biotechnology), and encouraged them to keep participating and working together as one.
