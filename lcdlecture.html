GUEST LECTURE: LIQUID CRYSTAL ENGINEERING A HUGE HIT AMONGST THE USS STUDENTS

Manu Kadyan

Wednesday, 8th October: A guest lecture on “Liquid Crystal Engineering” was held in the E-Block Auditorium at Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University, Dwarka. The lecture was held as part of Guest Lecture Series, a series of events organized by REACT, the official students’ organization of USCT.

The guest lecture was presented by Professor Hiroshi Yokoyama from the Liquid Crystal Institute, Kent State University (Ohio, USA). Professor Hiroshi Yokoyama is the current director of the Liquid Crystal Institute, Kent State University and one of the foremost experts in the field of Liquid Crystal Engineering.

Professor Yokoyama discussed the entire range of multidisciplinary topics associated with the science and technology of liquid crystals and related self-organized materials and devices. “The field of liquid crystals is now undergoing a quantum leap, beyond information displays into the advanced photonics, sensors, bio- and medical molecular devices, and smart materials for new energy applications”, he said while addressing the students.

Professor Yokoyama also enlightened the students about the various options available to students interested in pursuing a career in the field of liquid crystal engineering. “A series of pivotal contributions have been made by the LCI researchers, including the invention of the twisted nematic cell, the heart of LCDs” he informed the students while discussing the opportunities available for graduate students at the Liquid Crystal Institute of Kent State University. Professor Yokoyama shared valuable details about the Master’s program available at his institute and the requirements for admission into the program for engineering students.

The lecture was followed by an interactive Q&A session during which he highlighted the differences between the Liquid Crystal Displays and LEDs. He also discussed innovations such as the Google Glass which use liquid crystals and how liquid crystals have transformed our lives.

The guest lecture was a massive hit amongst the student community of Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University. The lecture witnessed an enthusiastic participation, with more than 120 students from the various University Schools of Studies attending the lecture. “I had never thought that the field of Liquid Crystal Engineering had so much potential! I will definitely explore the opportunities available in this field since I plan to pursue a career in Material Science.” said Shubham Mittal, a third year student from USCT. The event concluded with a vote of thanks given by Dr. Neeru Anand, Associate Professor, USCTacknowledged Professor Hiroshi Yokoyama for sharing his knowledge and research experiences with the students of GGSIPU. Professor Yokoyama later obliged for a few photographs with the students who attended the seminar and shared experiences about his college life with the students.
